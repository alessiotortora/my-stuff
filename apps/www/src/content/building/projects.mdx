# Projects — Built & In Progress

Over time, I've worked on various projects, both for clients and for myself. Here are a few of my favorites — a mix of things I've already built and what I'm currently working on. Each project has taught me something valuable.

<br />

## Ennio Grossi – Portfolio Website

[enniogrossi.com](https://www.enniogrossi.com)
<br />
This is the portfolio of a talented graphic designer. I really like this project because of its minimal design and simplicity. Ennio can easily add new projects, and they're automatically displayed — no extra effort needed.

We designed it together, and I built the site using [Next.js](https://nextjs.org/) for the frontend and [Sanity](https://www.sanity.io/) as the CMS. It's clean, fast, and fully editable.

<br />

## Aaron-Victor Peeters – Portfolio Website

[aaronvictorpeeters.com](https://www.aaronvictorpeeters.com/)
<br />
Aaron-Victor is a talented artist on the rise in Belgium. I built him a unique portfolio that showcases his projects as a stack you can flip through — closer to a piece of work than a typical site.

Built with [three.js](https://threejs.org/), [TanStack Start](https://tanstack.com/start), and [GSAP](https://gsap.com/) for the motion. The goal was something that feels as crafted as the work it presents.

<br />

## Personal Software

I've been building a lot of personal software lately — small tools and experiments to learn different things. With AI rising, this kind of building has become so much more fun: you can prototype an idea in an afternoon, learn something new, throw half of it away, and start again the next day.

A lot of what ends up shaping how I work professionally starts in one of these little side projects.

<br />

## Self-Hosting & Homelab

Running my own homelab has taught me more about Linux, networking, and how the internet actually works than any tutorial ever did. From Docker and reverse proxies to DNS, firewalls, and monitoring — it's all real once it's your machine and your downtime.

This site's analytics, for example, run entirely on hardware I manage. The hands-on side keeps me honest about performance, reliability, and what "production" actually means.

<br />

I'll continue updating this page as new projects go live or evolve. Each one is a step forward in refining how I design, build, and solve real-world problems on the web.
