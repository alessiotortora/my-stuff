# Use AI as a tool to be faster and smarter

The speed at which AI is rising is wild. Avoiding it isn't really an option anymore — every month the gap between people who use it well and people who don't gets a little wider.

I've been learning a lot with it, and using it daily. I build personal software with it, experiment with new stacks, prototype ideas I'd never get to otherwise. The learning curve on anything I'm curious about has collapsed: read, ask, build, repeat — all in the same afternoon.

But — use it as a **tool**, not as a brain. The trap is letting it overtake your decisions: architecture choices, library picks, the shape of an API. Speed without understanding is just debt that hasn't shown up yet.

So: keep thinking. Read the diff before you accept it. Ask why, not just how. Use the time AI gives you back to go deeper on the things that matter, not just to ship more shallow stuff.

That balance — fast on the boring parts, slow and careful on the parts that actually shape the thing — is what makes the difference now.
